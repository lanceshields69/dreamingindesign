The Problem

Talented designers
are getting passed
over for reasons
they'll never know.

The design job market is brutally competitive right now. Hundreds of applicants per role. Hiring managers spending 12 seconds per portfolio. And most designers have no idea what's actually working against them.

Peer feedback is too kind. Blog posts are too generic. Professional portfolio audits cost $999 and take a week. And recruiters never explain why they passed.

The result: designers keep applying, keep getting rejected, and keep making the same mistakes — because no one ever told them what to fix.

How it works.

Four steps from portfolio URL to a clear, actionable improvement plan.

01
Tell us about yourself

Role type, experience level, and the kind of company you're targeting. This context changes everything about what makes a great portfolio.

02
Submit your portfolio

Paste your URL, upload a PDF, or connect Behance or Dribbble. Password-protected? No problem — we've got you covered.

03
AI analyzes your work

Trained on real design leadership hiring criteria, the tool reads your portfolio the way a hiring manager actually does — fast, critical, and looking for specific signals.

04
Get your report

Scored across 5 dimensions with specific, actionable callouts. Not "improve your case studies" — but exactly what to change and why.

Biggest Opportunity
Your Fintech case study buries the most important decision on page 3.

Hiring managers at growth-stage companies aren't reading your whole case study. They're scanning for the moment where you made a hard call. Right now that moment is hidden behind two pages of research documentation that most won't reach.

Quick Wins — Fix This Week
  • 1
    Move your key design decision to the top of the case study — make it the lead, not the payoff.
  • 2

  • 3
    Your "About" page doesn't say what kind of role you're looking for. Add a single clear line.
  • 4
    Remove the student project from 2021 — it's lowering the overall signal of your work.
